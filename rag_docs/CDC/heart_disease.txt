Heart disease is a broad term describing a range of conditions that affect your heart. It is the leading cause of death for most racial and ethnic groups in the United States. The most common type is coronary artery disease (CAD), which involves the buildup of plaque in the arteries and can lead to chest pain (angina) or heart attacks. Other conditions include heart arrhythmias (irregular heartbeats) and heart failure.

Symptoms of a heart attack can include chest pain or discomfort, shortness of breath, weakness, light-headedness, nausea, or pain in the arms, shoulder, jaw, neck, or back. 

Major risk factors for heart disease include lifestyle behaviors such as an unhealthy diet (high in saturated fats, trans fat, and cholesterol), physical inactivity, tobacco use, and excessive alcohol consumption. Medical risk factors include high blood pressure, high cholesterol, diabetes, and obesity.

General prevention and management emphasize maintaining a healthy diet (low in salt, saturated fat, and trans fat; high in fiber), engaging in regular physical activity, quitting smoking, and working with a healthcare team to monitor and control blood pressure and cholesterol levels.

Urgent medical care is required immediately if you experience warning signs of a heart attack, such as severe chest pain, shortness of breath, or pain radiating down the arm. Call emergency services right away.
