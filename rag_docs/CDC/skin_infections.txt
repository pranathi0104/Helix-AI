Skin infections occur when bacteria, viruses, fungi, or parasites enter the skin and spread. They can range from mild, self-limiting conditions to severe, life-threatening diseases.

Common symptoms include redness, swelling, pain, pus or drainage, blistering, and sometimes a fever.

Major risk factors include poor hygiene, cuts or abrasions on the skin, a weakened immune system, and contact with an infected person or contaminated object.

General prevention involves washing hands often, keeping cuts and scrapes clean and covered until they heal, avoiding sharing personal items, and avoiding contact with people who have active skin infections.

Urgent medical care is needed if a skin infection is accompanied by a high fever, rapidly spreading redness or swelling, extreme pain, or if you have a weakened immune system.
