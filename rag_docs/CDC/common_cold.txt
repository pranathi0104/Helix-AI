The common cold is a viral infection of your nose and throat (upper respiratory tract). It is usually harmless, although it might not feel that way.

Common symptoms include a runny or stuffy nose, sore throat, cough, congestion, slight body aches or a mild headache, sneezing, and low-grade fever.

Risk factors include age (children are at greater risk), weakened immune system, time of year (fall and winter), and smoking.

Prevention includes washing your hands often with soap and water, avoiding close contact with people who are sick, and not touching your eyes, nose, or mouth with unwashed hands.

Urgent medical care is needed for adults if symptoms persist for more than 10 days, or if accompanied by a high fever, severe sinus pain, or extreme shortness of breath.
