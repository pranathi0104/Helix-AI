Arthritis means joint inflammation, but the term is used to describe around 200 conditions that affect joints, the tissues that surround the joint, and other connective tissue. It is a rheumatic disease.

Common symptoms include pain, aching, stiffness, and swelling in or around the joints. Some forms of arthritis, such as rheumatoid arthritis and lupus, can affect multiple organs and cause widespread symptoms.

Major risk factors include age, gender, genetics/inherited traits, overweight and obesity, joint injuries, and infection.

General prevention and management involve maintaining a healthy weight, staying physically active with low-impact exercises, protecting joints from injury, and consulting with a healthcare provider for appropriate treatment.

Urgent medical care is needed if you experience sudden, severe joint pain, swelling, and redness, which could indicate an infection or a condition like gout.
