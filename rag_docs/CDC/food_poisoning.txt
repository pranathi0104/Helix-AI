Food poisoning, also called foodborne illness, is illness caused by eating contaminated food. Infectious organisms or their toxins are the most common causes.

Common symptoms include upset stomach, stomach cramps, nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, and fever.

Major risk factors include eating raw or undercooked meats, unpasteurized milk, and food prepared by someone who has not washed their hands properly.

Prevention involves four simple steps: Clean (wash hands and surfaces often), Separate (do not cross-contaminate), Cook (cook to proper temperatures), and Chill (refrigerate promptly).

Urgent medical care is needed if you have a high fever, diarrhea lasting more than 3 days, bloody diarrhea, frequent vomiting, or signs of severe dehydration (like little to no urination, very dry mouth and throat, or feeling dizzy when standing up).
