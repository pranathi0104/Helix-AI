Sinusitis, or a sinus infection, occurs when fluid builds up in the air-filled pockets in the face (sinuses), allowing germs to grow.

Common symptoms include a runny or stuffy nose, facial pain or pressure, headache, sore throat, postnasal drip, and cough.

Major causes and risk factors include previous cold, seasonal allergies, smoking and exposure to secondhand smoke, and structural problems within the sinuses.

General prevention includes practicing good hand hygiene, getting recommended vaccines, avoiding close contact with people who have colds, and avoiding smoking.

Urgent medical care is needed if symptoms include severe headache or facial pain, symptoms that get worse after improving, fever lasting longer than 3-4 days, or multiple sinus infections in a year.
