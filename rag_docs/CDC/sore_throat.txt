A sore throat is pain, scratchiness or irritation of the throat that often worsens when you swallow. The most common cause is a viral infection, such as a cold or the flu.

Common symptoms include pain or a scratchy sensation in the throat, pain that worsens with swallowing or talking, and swollen, red tonsils.

Risk factors include being a child or teen, exposure to tobacco smoke, allergies, and exposure to chemical irritants.

Prevention includes good hygiene practices like washing hands, avoiding sharing food or utensils, and avoiding close contact with sick individuals. Gargling with warm salt water can help soothe the pain.

Urgent medical care is needed if a sore throat is severe, lasts longer than a week, or is accompanied by difficulty breathing, difficulty swallowing, or a rash.
