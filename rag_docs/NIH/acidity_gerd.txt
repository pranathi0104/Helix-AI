Gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD), often referred to as acidity or acid reflux, occurs when stomach acid frequently flows back into the tube connecting your mouth and stomach (esophagus).

Common symptoms include a burning sensation in your chest (heartburn), usually after eating, which might be worse at night; chest pain; difficulty swallowing; and regurgitation of food or sour liquid.

Risk factors include obesity, bulging of the top of the stomach up into the diaphragm (hiatal hernia), pregnancy, connective tissue disorders, and delayed stomach emptying.

Prevention and management involve maintaining a healthy weight, avoiding tight-fitting clothing, avoiding foods and drinks that trigger heartburn, eating smaller meals, and not lying down after a meal.

Urgent medical care is needed if you have severe chest pain or pressure, especially when combined with other signs and symptoms such as pain in the arm or jaw or difficulty breathing, as these could be signs of a heart attack.
