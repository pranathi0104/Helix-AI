Acne is a skin condition that occurs when your hair follicles become plugged with oil and dead skin cells. It causes whiteheads, blackheads or pimples.

Common symptoms include whiteheads, blackheads, small red, tender bumps (papules), pimples (pustules), and large, solid, painful lumps under the skin (nodules or cystic lesions).

Major causes include excess oil (sebum) production, hair follicles clogged by oil and dead skin cells, bacteria, and inflammation. Risk factors include hormonal changes, certain medications, diet, and stress.

General prevention and management involve washing your face twice daily with a gentle cleanser, avoiding harsh scrubs, keeping hair clean and off the face, and avoiding picking or squeezing blemishes.

Urgent medical care is not typically needed for acne, but consulting a dermatologist is recommended if over-the-counter products are ineffective or if acne is severe, painful, or causing scarring.
