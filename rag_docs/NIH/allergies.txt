Allergies occur when your immune system reacts to a foreign substance (allergen) such as pollen, bee venom, or pet dander, that does not cause a reaction in most people.

Common symptoms vary depending on the allergen and can include sneezing, runny or stuffy nose, itchy and watery eyes, rash, hives, or swelling.

Risk factors include having a family history of asthma or allergies, being a child, and having asthma or another allergic condition.

General prevention involves avoiding known triggers, keeping a diary to track symptoms and activities, and wearing a medical alert bracelet if you have had a severe allergic reaction.

Urgent medical care is needed for signs of anaphylaxis, a life-threatening reaction that includes severe shortness of breath, lightheadedness, a rapid, weak pulse, or loss of consciousness.
