Gastritis is a general term for a group of conditions with one thing in common: inflammation of the lining of the stomach.

Common symptoms include a gnawing or burning ache or pain (indigestion) in your upper abdomen that may become either worse or better with eating; nausea; vomiting; and a feeling of fullness in your upper abdomen after eating.

Major risk factors include bacterial infection (H. pylori), regular use of pain relievers, older age, excessive alcohol use, and stress.

Prevention involves avoiding triggers like alcohol, spicy foods, or certain medications, and practicing good hygiene to prevent infections.

Urgent medical care is needed if you have severe abdominal pain, vomiting that does not stop, or if you are vomiting blood or have black, tarry stools, which can indicate stomach bleeding.
