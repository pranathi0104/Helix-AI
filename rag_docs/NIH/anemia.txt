Anemia is a condition in which you lack enough healthy red blood cells to carry adequate oxygen to your body's tissues. Having anemia can make you feel tired and weak.

Common symptoms include fatigue, weakness, pale or yellowish skin, irregular heartbeats, shortness of breath, dizziness, and cold hands and feet.

Major risk factors include a diet lacking in certain vitamins and minerals, intestinal disorders, menstruation, pregnancy, and chronic conditions.

General prevention and management involve eating a healthy diet rich in iron, folate, and vitamin B12, and taking supplements if prescribed by a doctor.

Urgent medical care is needed if you experience extreme fatigue, shortness of breath, or a rapid, irregular heartbeat, as these can be signs of severe anemia or other serious conditions.
