A fever is a temporary increase in your body temperature, often due to an illness. Having a fever is a sign that something out of the ordinary is going on in your body.

Common symptoms that may accompany a fever include sweating, chills and shivering, headache, muscle aches, loss of appetite, irritability, dehydration, and general weakness.

Causes include viral or bacterial infections, heat exhaustion, certain inflammatory conditions, and sometimes medications or immunizations.

Management includes resting and drinking plenty of fluids. Medication like acetaminophen or ibuprofen can help lower the fever if it is causing discomfort.

Urgent medical care is needed if a fever is accompanied by a severe headache, stiff neck, unexplained skin rash, mental confusion, persistent vomiting, or difficulty breathing.
