Back pain is one of the most common medical problems, affecting 8 out of 10 people at some point during their lives. It can range from a dull, constant ache to a sudden, sharp pain.

Common symptoms include muscle aching, shooting or stabbing pain, pain that radiates down your leg, and pain that worsens with bending, lifting, standing or walking.

Causes and risk factors include age, lack of exercise, excess weight, diseases like arthritis and cancer, improper lifting, and psychological conditions like depression and anxiety.

Prevention involves regular low-impact aerobic activities, building muscle strength and flexibility, maintaining a healthy weight, and practicing good posture.

Urgent medical care is needed if back pain causes new bowel or bladder problems, is accompanied by a fever, follows a fall or blow to your back, or if you experience weakness, numbness, or tingling in one or both legs.
