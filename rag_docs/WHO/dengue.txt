Dengue is a mosquito-borne viral infection causing a severe flu-like illness and, sometimes causing a potentially lethal complication called severe dengue.

Common symptoms include a sudden high fever, severe headache, pain behind the eyes, severe joint and muscle pain, fatigue, nausea, vomiting, and a skin rash.

Risk factors include living or traveling in tropical and subtropical areas, and a prior infection with a dengue virus.

Prevention involves mosquito control, using mosquito repellents, wearing protective clothing, and eliminating standing water where mosquitoes breed.

Urgent medical care is needed if you develop severe abdominal pain, persistent vomiting, bleeding gums or nose, blood in urine or stools, or difficulty breathing, as these are signs of severe dengue.
