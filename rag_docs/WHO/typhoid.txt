Typhoid fever is a life-threatening infection caused by the bacterium Salmonella Typhi. It is usually spread through contaminated food or water.

Common symptoms include a prolonged high fever, fatigue, headache, nausea, abdominal pain, and constipation or diarrhea. Some patients may have a rash.

Risk factors include lack of access to safe water and adequate sanitation, and travel to areas where typhoid fever is endemic.

Prevention involves drinking safe water, improving sanitation, good hygiene practices, and vaccination for those traveling to high-risk areas.

Urgent medical care is needed if you suspect you have typhoid fever, as it requires treatment with antibiotics. Severe cases can lead to serious complications or death.
