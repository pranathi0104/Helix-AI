Malaria is a life-threatening disease caused by parasites that are transmitted to people through the bites of infected female Anopheles mosquitoes.

Common symptoms include fever, chills, and flu-like illness. Left untreated, they may develop severe complications and die.

Risk factors include living or traveling in areas where malaria is prevalent, particularly in parts of Africa, South Asia, and South America.

Prevention involves using insecticide-treated mosquito nets, indoor residual spraying, taking antimalarial medications before, during, and after travel to high-risk areas, and using insect repellent.

Urgent medical care is needed if you experience a fever, chills, and flu-like illness, especially if you have recently traveled to an area where malaria is common. It is a medical emergency that requires immediate treatment.
