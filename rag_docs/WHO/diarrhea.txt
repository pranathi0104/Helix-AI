Diarrheal disease is characterized by the passage of three or more loose or liquid stools per day (or more frequent passage than is normal for the individual).

Common symptoms include frequent loose, watery stools, abdominal cramps, fever, bloating, and sometimes nausea.

Major causes include infections (bacterial, viral, and parasitic), malnutrition, and contaminated food or water sources.

Prevention involves access to safe drinking water, improved sanitation, good personal and food hygiene, and exclusive breastfeeding for the first six months of life.

Urgent medical care is needed if diarrhea lasts more than 2 days in adults (or 24 hours in children), or if accompanied by severe dehydration, severe abdominal or rectal pain, bloody or black stools, or a fever above 102 F (39 C).
