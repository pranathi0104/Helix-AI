Headaches are one of the most common physical complaints, often presenting as pain in the head or face. A migraine is a severe type of headache usually felt as a throbbing pain on one side of the head, often accompanied by nausea, vomiting, and sensitivity to light and sound.

Common symptoms include head pain, visual disturbances (aura) for migraines, nausea, and sensitivity to stimuli.

Risk factors include stress, lack of sleep, dehydration, and certain trigger foods.

Prevention involves managing stress, staying hydrated, keeping a regular sleep schedule, and avoiding known triggers. Pain relievers can help manage symptoms.

Urgent medical care is needed if a headache is sudden and extremely severe, accompanied by fever, stiff neck, confusion, seizures, or weakness on one side of the face or body.
