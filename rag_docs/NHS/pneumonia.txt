Pneumonia is an infection that inflames the air sacs in one or both lungs. The air sacs may fill with fluid or pus, causing a cough, fever, chills, and difficulty breathing. It can be caused by bacteria, viruses, or fungi. 

Common symptoms include a cough (which may produce thick mucus), fever, sweating and shivering chills, shortness of breath, rapid shallow breathing, and sharp or stabbing chest pain that gets worse when you breathe deeply or cough. In older adults, symptoms may be milder or include sudden confusion.

Major risk factors include being very young (infants and young children) or over age 65, having a weakened immune system, smoking, and having chronic medical conditions like asthma, COPD, or heart disease. 

Prevention involves practicing good hygiene, quitting smoking (which damages the lungs' natural defenses), and getting vaccinated. Vaccines are available for certain types of pneumonia, as well as for influenza and COVID-19, which can lead to pneumonia.

Urgent medical care is needed if you experience severe difficulty breathing, chest pain, a persistent high fever, or confusion. Pneumonia can be life-threatening, particularly in high-risk groups, and often requires medical treatment such as antibiotics (for bacterial pneumonia) or hospital care.
