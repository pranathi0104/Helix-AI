Constipation is a common condition that affects people of all ages. It means that you are not passing stools (poo) regularly or you are unable to completely empty your bowel.

Common symptoms include passing fewer than three stools a week, having lumpy or hard stools, straining to have bowel movements, and feeling as though there is a blockage in your rectum.

Risk factors include a low-fiber diet, lack of physical activity, not drinking enough fluids, and certain medications or medical conditions.

Prevention involves eating a high-fiber diet, drinking plenty of water, and staying active.

Urgent medical care is needed if constipation is accompanied by severe abdominal pain, blood in your stool, unexplained weight loss, or if it is a new and persistent problem.
