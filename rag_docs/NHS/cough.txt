A cough is a reflex action to clear your airways of mucus and irritants such as dust or smoke. It is a common symptom of upper respiratory tract infections.

Common symptoms include a tickle in the throat, chest discomfort, and coughing up phlegm or mucus (productive cough) or no mucus (dry cough).

Causes include viral infections like the cold or flu, smoking, allergies, asthma, and acid reflux.

Prevention and management involve staying hydrated, using a humidifier, resting, and avoiding irritants like smoke.

Urgent medical care is needed if a cough lasts more than 3 weeks, you cough up blood, have chest pain, or experience difficulty breathing.
