Atopic eczema (atopic dermatitis) is the most common form of eczema, a condition that causes the skin to become itchy, dry and cracked.

Common symptoms include dry, itchy skin, redness and inflammation, and skin that is cracked, weeping, or bleeding.

Risk factors include a family history of eczema, asthma, or hay fever, and exposure to certain environmental triggers like soaps, detergents, stress, and weather changes.

General prevention and management involve keeping the skin moisturized with emollients, avoiding known triggers, using mild soaps, and avoiding scratching.

Urgent medical care is needed if the skin becomes infected (e.g., increased redness, swelling, pus, or a yellow crust), or if the eczema is severe and affecting sleep or daily activities.
